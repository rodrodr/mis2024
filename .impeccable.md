## Design Context

### Product
- Municipal Ideology Explorer is a public-facing research interface for exploring Brazilian municipal ideology from 1994 to 2024.
- Primary users are academically oriented, but the interface should remain legible to journalists and policy-adjacent readers.
- The product tone is serious, evidence-led, and editorial rather than bureaucratic or over-academic.

### Visual Language
- Preserve the existing warm paper-like background and editorial contrast.
- Keep `Newsreader` as the display/headline voice and `Inter` as the UI label voice for now.
- The aesthetic direction is editorial data-journalism: strong hierarchy, restrained color, meaningful graphics, minimal ornament.

### Color System
- Backgrounds are warm neutrals, not pure white.
- Primary green is the anchor for main narrative/data emphasis.
- Secondary blue can support analytical layers.
- Ideology uses diverging colors already established in the app:
  - left: `#B22222`
  - center: `#7FB069`
  - right: `#0047AB`
- Avoid decorative gradients and avoid inventing new bright palette families.

### Layout Principles
- Do not default to generic card grids as the main organizing pattern.
- Use a narrative top section and then analytical sections with clear rhythm.
- Prefer asymmetry and editorial pacing over perfectly repetitive blocks.
- Dense analytical pages may be more compact than the landing, but still need hierarchy and breathing room.

### Interaction Principles
- Primary interaction should be obvious and low-friction.
- Hover can enrich charts, but essential information must remain visible without hover.
- Comparison tools can be secondary, revealed within the main analytical flow.
- Motion should stay subtle and functional.

### Accessibility and Responsiveness
- Support `pt`, `en`, and `es`; layouts must tolerate longer translated strings.
- Mobile views should preserve hierarchy, not just stack every block identically.
- Maintain clear contrast and avoid small, low-contrast annotation text for critical information.

### Project-Specific Avoidances
- No mock or decorative data visualizations pretending to be analytical.
- No academic-paper stiffness in top-level pages.
- No oversized accent borders doing structural work.
- No hero sections or dashboards made of interchangeable boxes with repeated messaging.
