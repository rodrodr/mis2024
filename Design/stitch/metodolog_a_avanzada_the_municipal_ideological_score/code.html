Methodology & Empirical Rigor

The Municipal Ideological Score represents a systematic effort to quantify political sentiment across Brazilian municipalities. This document outlines the scientific foundation, outlining data provenance, bayesian scaling techniques, and the construction of key electoral indicators.

Data Provenance

Primary Source

Tribunal Superior Eleitoral (TSE)

Raw Data Acquisition & Cleaning

Our analysis relies exclusively on raw electoral microdata provided by the TSE. The dataset spans an exhaustive timeframe from 1994 to 2026, capturing decades of municipal electoral cycles.

The cleaning process involves reconciling municipal boundary changes over the 30-year period, standardizing party acronyms (which frequently undergo institutional transformations), and treating missing or annulled votes to ensure a consistent baseline for calculating effective vote shares. Anomalous entries resulting from suspended candidacies are systematically isolated prior to scaling.

Analytical Models

Scaling Protocol

Bayesian Ideology Scaling

To estimate the ideological position of municipalities, we deploy a Bayesian spatial model. By treating party coalitions and mayoral alignments as observable manifestations of underlying latent ideology, the model generates posterior distributions of municipal preferences. This probabilistic approach allows us to quantify uncertainty inherently present in local electoral outcomes.

Fragmentation Metric

Effective Number of Parties (NEP)

We calculate the NEP using the Laakso-Taagepera index. This metric moves beyond simply counting active parties, weighting them by their respective vote shares. It provides a robust indicator of the competitive landscape and institutional fragmentation at the municipal level during each electoral cycle.

Key Indicators Dictionary

Ideological Polarization

The distance between the ideological estimates of the top two contending coalitions. Higher values indicate severe municipal division along the estimated left-right spatial axis.

Fragmentation Index

Derived directly from the NEP calculation. It measures the probability that two randomly selected voters in a municipality chose different political parties in the mayoral race.

Electoral Competitiveness

The inverse of the margin of victory between the first and second-place candidates. Indicates the degree of political contestation and vulnerability of the incumbent coalition.
